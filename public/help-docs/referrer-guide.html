Refer, track, and earn

Everything you need to send a referral, follow it through to Deed Issued, and see how you rank — for everyone who refers through the portal.

What this is

When a tenant can't pass referencing or provide their own guarantor, you can refer them to opndoor's professional guarantor service in a couple of minutes. opndoor provides a Deed of Guarantee in favour of the property so the let can go ahead instead of falling through.

This portal is where you send those referrals and track each one. You see your own referrals — every application you've sent, from Sent through to Deed Issued.

At a glance
  • You refer a tenant who can't pass referencing.
  • The tenant pays a one-off fee of one month's rent.
  • opndoor issues the Deed of Guarantee and you track it to completion.
  • You never enter the guarantee reference, issue date or expiry — they're assigned automatically.

Signing in with two-factor

The portal holds live tenant data, so every account signs in with two-factor authentication.

  1. Enter your work email and password.
  2. Enter the 6-digit code from your authenticator app.
  3. The first time, you'll scan a QR code to enrol your authenticator — keep that app; you'll use it every sign-in.

Lost your authenticator? Ask your manager or opndoor to reset it — you'll set up a new one at your next sign-in.

Sending a referral

Open New application and complete the sections in order:

  1. Tenant — name, contact details and date of birth.
  2. Property — the address being let.
  3. Tenancy — the monthly rent and the tenancy start date.
  4. Agent & branch — search for your agency and branch, or add a new one on the fly if it isn't listed.

Submit to send the referral to the tenant. The tenant pays the one-month fee through a secure link; once paid, opndoor issues the deed.

Adding an agency or branch on the fly

If your agency or branch isn't in the list, add it right on the form. It's attached to your referral immediately and sent to opndoor to confirm as a canonical record (they check for duplicates). You don't wait on that — your referral goes ahead.

Tracking your applications

The Applications screen lists your referrals. Each moves through the same lifecycle:

StatusWhat it means
SentThe referral is with the tenant to pay the fee.
PaidThe one-month guarantor fee has been paid.
Deed IssuedThe Deed of Guarantee has been issued and stored on the record.
Withdrawn / ExpiredThe referral was withdrawn, or expired unpaid — terminal states.

Filter by status, agency or branch, search by tenant or reference, and narrow to a time period. Open any row for the full detail, including the deed once it's issued.

Your League ranking

The League shows how you rank among referrers at your partner, by referrals sent in the selected period. If your partner keeps the referrer leaderboard private, you'll see only your own performance rather than the full table.
